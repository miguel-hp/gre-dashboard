Stay in the Fight

Strategies to make studying stick -- especially on hard days.

The secret of getting ahead is getting started.
-- MARK TWAIN
🧱
Stack, Don't Skip
Missing one day is an accident. Missing two is the start of a habit. On low-energy days, do just the 20-min vocab session. Keep the chain alive.
📍
Anchor to a Place
Pick one spot that is only for GRE. Not your bed, not the couch. That physical anchor trains your brain to switch into study mode the moment you sit down.
⏱
The 5-Minute Rule
Tell yourself: "I'll just do 5 minutes." You almost never stop at 5. The hardest part is always starting -- once the engine is running, momentum takes over.
🎯
Score on the Scoreboard
Log your practice scores -- even bad ones. Seeing measurable progress (from 145 → 152 → 160) is the most powerful motivator there is. Numbers don't lie.
🔊
Make It Loud
Tell one person about your goal every week. Accountability to others creates pressure that self-discipline alone can't match. Say it out loud: "I'm going to Booth."
🖼
Visualize the Letter
Close your eyes for 60 seconds. You open your email. It says "Congratulations." Feel it completely. Then open your books. That feeling is what you're studying for.
🌙
Protect Your Sleep
Sleep is when your brain consolidates what it learned. Sacrificing sleep for more study time is a bad trade. 7-8 hours makes every hour of studying 40% more effective.
📅
Beast Mode is Coming
From June 15 onward, you shift into full gear. The "light" period until then is about building the habit -- so that when beast mode starts, it's automatic, not a shock.
"You are not studying for the GRE.
You are building the version of yourself
that belongs at Booth and Kellogg."
REMEMBER THIS EVERY SINGLE DAY
